What Is Better Jobs Ontario?

Better Jobs Ontario is a government-funded training program designed to help unemployed or underemployed individuals gain new skills and return to the workforce. The program provides financial assistance so participants can receive training for occupations with strong employment prospects.

The goal of the program is to help individuals:

  • Develop new job-ready skills
  • Train for in-demand occupations
  • Return to employment as quickly as possible

Funding may cover both training costs and certain living expenses while you study.

How Much Funding Can You Receive?

Eligible applicants may receive up to $28,000 for training programs lasting 1 year or less. Funding is non-reimbursable.

Training Costs That May Be Covered:

  • Tuition
  • Books and learning materials
  • Certification exam fees
  • Required equipment and supplies
  • Student fees

Additional Support Available:

  • Transportation
  • Basic living expenses
  • Childcare
  • Disability-related support
  • Living-away-from-home costs (if applicable)

Who May Qualify for BJO Funding?

Laid-Off Workers — you may be eligible if you:

  • Were laid off from your job
  • Are unemployed or underemployed
  • Are working temporary or part-time jobs

Low-Income Individuals — you may also qualify if you:

  • Have been unemployed for 12 weeks or longer
  • Are part of a low-income household
  • Need new training to find stable employment

All applicants must also:

  • Live in Ontario
  • Be legally authorized to work in Canada
  • Demonstrate that the training leads to good employment opportunities

Why Many Applicants Need Guidance

The Better Jobs Ontario application requires several steps and supporting documentation — including employment assessments, job market research, training plans, and more. Many applicants find the process difficult to navigate on their own.

Metro College has more than 15 years of experience assisting applicants in preparing successful Better Jobs Ontario applications. Our advisors can help you:

  • Understand eligibility and suitability requirements
  • Select appropriate training programs
  • Prepare your training plan
  • Understand the full application process

How the Application Process Works

  1. Meet with an Employment Ontario Advisor

    Begin by meeting with a local Employment Ontario service provider who will assess your employment situation.

  2. Develop an Employment Action Plan

    The advisor determines whether training is the best path to employment and helps create a formal employment plan.

  3. Prepare the BJO Application

    Prepare all required documents including: training program details, job search records, résumé, employment history, and labour market information.

  4. Ministry Review and Approval

    The Ministry reviews the application and decides whether funding will be approved. If approved, you can begin training right away.
